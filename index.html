Dear Notes

Dear Notes is an independent project exploring knowledge management from many angles: as a strategic tool for companies, as a thinking practice, and as a relationship with what we (don't) know.

Through writing, workshops, and one-on-one work, I help people and organizations build sustainable ways to capture, organize, and use what they learn—not as productivity theater, but as a practice for living with ideas over time.

Este es un espacio de escritura pública donde las ideas crecen, se cruzan y cambian de forma: un espacio vivo y en transformación, distinto de un blog organizado cronológicamente o de un archivo cerrado. Por eso hablo de jardín digital, porque se parece más a cultivar que a publicar.

Al igual que en un jardín físico, aquí importa el cuidado constante: sembrar ideas, regarlas con lecturas, podarlas con revisiones y aceptar que crezcan de forma desigual y a su propio ritmo.

En un museo predominan las obras acabadas, en una biblioteca, el orden y el archivo; en un mapa, los caminos ya trazados. Un jardín digital, en cambio, responde a otra lógica: es proceso más que resultado.

❀ Una forma de relación con el conocimiento: se trata de una relación íntima y pública a la vez. Como en todo jardín, no todo florece, pero en esa imperfección está su valor.

✤ Diferencias con un blog: los blogs suelen estar organizados en entradas lineales y fechadas, aquí el crecimiento es no cronológico y ramificado. Mientras el blog funciona como un diario público, el jardín es un laboratorio personal en proceso.

✿ Cómo leer este jardín: no es un espacio lineal ni cerrado; puedes empezar por cualquier nota y dejarte guiar por los enlaces. Algunas páginas están más completas y otras son solo apuntes. Todo forma parte del proceso. Volver a una idea con el tiempo significa encontrarla cambiada o conectada de otra manera.

About Me

A Bit of Background

I grew up in Málaga, Spain, in the 1990s—in a flat with high ceilings near the sea and the English Cemetery. Art and making things were part of daily life. My childhood was filled with mountains, reading, playing with my siblings and cousins, bougainvillea, and bicycles.

When I turned 10, my siblings gifted me my first computer, along with the Encarta 2000 encyclopedia on CD-ROM. Between Word, those discs, and all the books at home, I spent my time writing stories and inventing worlds. I haven't stopped reading, writing, or learning new things since.

I studied Translation and Interpreting (German and English) in Granada. After graduating, I moved to Münster, Germany, where I've lived since 2012.

My career has wound through translation, localization, content strategy, and knowledge work. I was Language Lead at Notion from 2020 to 2024. In 2022 and 2023, I worked as Course Manager for Tiago Forte's Building a Second Brain at Forte Labs. Before that, I spent five years at Johnson & Johnson MedTech and worked as a freelance translator and teacher.

By day, I am lucky to work as UX Content and Localization Manager at Trusted Shops, a German e-commerce company, with a fantastic team, where I focus on our (international) customers by ensuring content works seamlessly and resonates across languages and markets.

What I Care About

I care and think about personal knowledge management A LOT. How do we capture what matters? How do we let ideas develop without forcing them? How do we build systems that support actual thinking, not just the appearance of productivity?

My newsletter, notes and conversations—they're all experiments in using writing to figure out what I actually think.

I live in Münster with my husband and son in a housing project where we are working toward more sustainable ways of living together.
