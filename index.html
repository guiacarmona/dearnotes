About Me

Hello! I'm Guia, I translate, write, learn, and teach about everything that fascinates me. Curiosity has been my greatest ally, and making space for it has allowed me to explore many paths.

What I Do

I created [Dear Notes](link), an independent project exploring knowledge management from many angles: as a strategic tool for companies, as a thinking practice, and as a relationship with what we (don't) know. Through writing, workshops, and one-on-one work, I help people and organizations build sustainable ways to capture, organize, and use what they learn—not as productivity theater, but as a practice for living with ideas over time. By day, I am lucky to work as UX Content and Localization Manager at Trusted Shops, a German e-commerce company, with a fantastic team, where I focus on our (international) customers by ensuring content works seamlessly and resonates across languages and markets.

A Bit of Background

I grew up in Málaga, Spain, in the 1990s—in a flat with high ceilings near the sea and the English Cemetery. Art and making things were part of daily life. My childhood was filled with mountains, reading, playing with my siblings and cousins, bougainvillea, and bicycles. When I turned 10, my siblings gifted me my first computer, along with the Encarta 2000 encyclopedia on CD-ROM. Between Word, those discs, and all the books at home, I spent my time writing stories and inventing worlds. I haven't stopped reading, writing, or learning new things since. I studied Translation and Interpreting (German and English) in Granada. After graduating, I moved to Münster, Germany, where I've lived since 2012. My career has wound through translation, localization, content strategy, and knowledge work. I was Language Lead at Notion from 2020 to 2024. In 2022 and 2023, I worked as Course Manager for Tiago Forte's *Building a Second Brain* at Forte Labs. Before that, I spent five years at Johnson & Johnson MedTech and worked as a freelance translator and teacher.

What I Care About

I care and think about personal knowledge management A LOT. How do we capture what matters? How do we let ideas develop without forcing them? How do we build systems that support actual thinking, not just the appearance of productivity? My newsletter, notes and conversations—they're all experiments in using writing to figure out what I actually think. I live in Münster with my husband and son in a housing project where we are working toward more sustainable ways of living together.
